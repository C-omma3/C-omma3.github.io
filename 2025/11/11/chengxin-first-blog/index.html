Zhang chengxin：a Linguist and Musician from China

Hello, I am Zhang Chengxin. I am currently a graduate student in Linguistics at Beijing Normal University, having completed my undergraduate studies in Chinese Language and Literature at the College of Liberal Arts, Nankai University. My research focuses on (generative) syntax, semantics, morphology, and their interfaces, with specific interests in event structure, argument structure, converbs, negation, case, and agreement. I am also deeply interested in mathematical logic, cognitive science, and computational linguistics. Currently, I am compiling an open-access LaTeX introductory tutorial on linguistics in Chinese for students in China, which will later be uploaded to this website.

I have a strong interest in a wide range of languages. I believe that uncovering the deep nature of language requires expanding our research sample. Through scientific comparison, we can explore the universals of human language and test the notion of Universal Grammar (UG) as advocated by Chomsky. Therefore, my research covers languages from various families, including Mandarin Chinese, Jin dialects, English, Manchu, Sibe, and Tibetan, among others. My current research focuses primarily on the converb constructions in Manchu-Tungusic languages and their effects on argument structure and event structure. I also maintain a long-standing and keen interest in the semantic contributions of morphology.

Outside of linguistics, I am a music enthusiast and a semi-professional performer of Shaanbei Storytelling (a traditional northern Chinese folk art). I study under the renowned artist Xiong Zhuying, who provided voice work for the AAA video game Black Myth: Wukong. Additionally, I have a passion for classical music and am currently dedicated to learning the piano.
